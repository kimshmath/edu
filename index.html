Demonstration Projects

Interactive explorations where abstract mathematics becomes something you can see, hear, and touch. Each project synthesizes its content in real time — no recordings, no pre-rendered assets. Just math.

01 Banach–Tarski: F₂ on S² →

The most counterintuitive theorem in mathematics, visualized. Watch the free group F₂ act on the sphere via rotations, and see how a ball can be decomposed and reassembled into two identical copies of itself.

Free Groups Paradoxical Decomposition 3D Visualization
02 Can You Hear the Shape of a Drum? →

Mark Kac's famous 1966 question, made audible. Strike virtual drums, see their vibrational modes as Bessel-function eigenstates, and hear why two differently shaped drums can produce identical spectra.

Spectral Geometry Bessel Functions Eigenvalues Modal Synthesis
02k 드럼의 모양을 들을 수 있을까?  Korean edition →

Mark Kac의 1966년 질문 — 스펙트럼 기하학, 고유모드, GWW 드럼을 인터랙티브로 탐험합니다. Korean-language version of the drum shape problem.

한국어 Spectral Geometry Bessel Functions Modal Synthesis
03 The Mathematics of Sound →

From Pythagoras to Fourier to your smartphone. Build waveforms from harmonics, compare tuning systems across instruments, and explore how the cochlea performs frequency analysis — all with live-synthesized audio.

Fourier Analysis Temperament Acoustics Web Audio
03k 소리의 수학  Korean edition →

피타고라스에서 푸리에, 스마트폰까지. 배음 합성기, 조율법 비교, 달팽이관 시뮬레이터를 한국어로 탐험합니다. Korean-language version of the mathematics of sound.

한국어 Fourier Analysis Temperament Acoustics
04 Living in a Hyperbolic Space →

Where the parallel postulate fails and triangles contain less than 180°. Walk through hyperbolic 3-space, tile the infinite plane, explore Teichmüller space, and follow Mirzakhani's path from geodesics to the Witten conjecture.

Hyperbolic Geometry Tessellations Teichmüller Space Mirzakhani
