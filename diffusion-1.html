02 — THE EQUATION

One Equation Rules Them All

Joseph Fourier discovered this in 1822 while studying heat flow through metal. What he found was shockingly general — the same equation governs the spread of heat, the diffusion of molecules, the flow of information through networks, even the pricing of financial options.

∂u/∂t = α ∇²u

In words: the rate of change at any point is proportional to how different that point is from its neighbors. Hot spots cool. Concentrations dilute. Peaks flatten. The constant α controls the speed.

Each pixel's temperature changes based on the difference between it and its neighbors: u(x, t+dt) = u(x,t) + α·dt·(u(x+1,t) − 2u(x,t) + u(x−1,t))/dx². The sharp peak diffuses into a wider, lower Gaussian — concentrations always spread, and total "heat" is conserved.

03 — THE FUNDAMENTAL SOLUTION

The Gaussian Emerges

If you start with all the heat concentrated at a single point, the solution to the heat equation is beautiful and exact:

Φ(x, t) = 1 / √(4παt) · exp(−x² / 4αt)

This is the Gaussian — the bell curve — born not from statistics, but from physics. The width grows as √t, and the peak height drops as 1/√t. Energy is conserved: the area under the curve is always exactly 1.

04 — THE MICROSCOPIC VIEW

Random Walks and Brownian Motion

Zoom into the ink drop. Each molecule bounces randomly off its neighbors — a random walk. In 1905, Einstein showed that this microscopic chaos produces macroscopic diffusion. The key insight:

⟨x²⟩ = 2Dt

The average squared displacement grows linearly with time. After N steps, a particle is typically √N steps from where it started — not N. This is why diffusion is slow: doubling the distance takes four times as long.

The deep connection — The heat equation and random walks are two faces of the same coin. At the macroscopic level, we see smooth diffusion governed by ∂u/∂t = α∇²u. At the microscopic level, countless random walkers produce a Gaussian distribution that satisfies the exact same equation. Einstein used this connection to prove that atoms exist — and won a Nobel Prize for it.

05 — TWO EQUATIONS, TWO WORLDS

Waves vs Diffusion

These two equations look almost identical — but they describe fundamentally different universes:

∂²u/∂t² = c² ∇²u
Wave equation
∂u/∂t = α ∇²u
Heat equation

A wave preserves shape — it propagates without losing information. Diffusion destroys information — it smears, smooths, and forgets. One is reversible; the other is not.

Side by Side

Same initial pulse, same spatial setup — watch how differently they evolve.

WAVE — ∂²u/∂t² = c²∇²u
DIFFUSION — ∂u/∂t = α∇²u

Wave Equation

Second-order in time. Information travels at speed c without distortion. Reversible — you can run the clock backward and recover the original pulse. Governs sound, light, and seismic waves.

Heat / Diffusion Equation

First-order in time. Information spreads outward and loses its shape. Irreversible — you cannot "un-diffuse" a dissolved drop of ink. Governs heat, chemical concentration, and probability.
